Srinath Setty


I am a Researcher at Microsoft Research with research interests in distributed systems, security, and privacy. My current projects focus on proving the correct execution of stateful and concurrent services in zero-knowledge, distributed ledgers and blockchains, and unobservable communication. I got my Ph.D. in computer science from UT Austin where I co-founded the Pepper project, which ignited a thriving research area to refine and implement (zero-knowledge) argument protocols based on the theory of probabilistically checkable proofs (PCPs). Here is a copy of my CV. At MSR, I have worked with the following excellent interns: Jonathan Bootle (2018), Kirill Nikitin (2018), Willy R. Vasquez (2018), Sebastian Angel (2017), Tyler Hunt (2017), Bernhard Kragl (2017), Kevin Sekniqi (2017), Soumya Basu (2016), Ashay Rane (2016), Hao Chen (2015-16), and Chunzhi Su (2015).
